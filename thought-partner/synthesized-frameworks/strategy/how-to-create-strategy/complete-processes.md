# Complete Strategy Creation Processes

## Purpose

Complete strategy creation processes provide end-to-end methodologies for building product strategy. They guide teams through the full journey from understanding the current situation to creating an actionable strategic plan.

## When to Use

- When creating a new product strategy from scratch
- When you need a structured approach to strategy work
- To guide a strategy planning process over weeks/months
- When you want to ensure all critical elements are covered
- For major strategy revisions or pivots

## Key Processes

### 7-Step Strategy Playbook (Aakash Gupta)

**Goal**: Create clear, adaptable strategies in less than a day (refine over a week/month)

**7 Steps**:
1. **Objective**: Define measurable mission (Mission + Measure)
2. **Users**: Understand jobs-to-be-done and needs
3. **Superpowers**: Identify unfair advantages (7 Powers)
4. **Vision**: Show future state visually
5. **Pillars**: Choose 2-4 strategic bets
6. **Impact**: Quantify business outcomes and ROI
7. **Roadmap**: Focus on problems & outcomes

**Process Phases**:
- **1 Day (Snap Strategy)**: Capture best first draft
- **1 Week (Strategy Sprint)**: Validate and align
- **1 Month (Validated Strategy)**: Deep validation + storytelling

**Key Principles**:
- Simple > complex
- Show > tell (visualize strategy)
- Measure outcomes, not outputs
- Continuous > static
- Alignment > perfection

### Your Strategy Planning Process (Digible)

**Goal**: Comprehensive 4-month process to create EPD strategy

**Two Main Components**:

1. **Diagnosis** (6 weeks): Diagnose business, users, market
   - Mind-Meld & Shitty First Draft (2 weeks)
   - Codify Beliefs/Diagnosis (4 weeks)
     - Customer Diagnosis
     - Business Diagnosis
     - Market Diagnosis

2. **Vision & Strategy** (9 weeks): Define where you want to end up and how to get there
   - Strategy & Vision - Core (3 weeks)
     - User Segmentation/Distribution
     - User Needs
     - Strategic Themes
     - Differentiation/Tactics
     - Vision No-Fi Flow
   - Strategy & Vision - Final (4 weeks)
     - Vision-type
     - Roadmap
     - Resourcing
     - Metrics
     - Business Impact
     - Key Risks
   - Sign-off & Road-show (2 weeks)

**Key Principles**:
- Non-linear process (activities bleed into each other)
- Iterative refinement
- Stakeholder involvement throughout
- Visual and tangible outputs

### HBS JTBD Strategy Creation Process

**Goal**: Use Jobs-to-Be-Done to create strategy

**6 Steps**:
1. **Interviews**: Conduct JTBD interviews
2. **Extract Pushes & Pulls**: Identify forces driving customer decisions
3. **Count Pushes & Pulls**: Quantify frequency/importance
4. **Cluster Under Themes**: Group into strategic pillars
5. **Market Map**: Map pillars by importance vs alternatives
6. **Features**: Define features for each pillar

**Key Principles**:
- Start with customer jobs, not solutions
- Use quantitative analysis of qualitative insights
- Cluster insights into strategic themes
- Map competitive landscape

## Key Principles

- **Start with diagnosis**: Understand current situation before creating strategy
- **Iterative process**: Strategy improves through multiple rounds of refinement
- **Stakeholder involvement**: Get input from key people throughout
- **Visual outputs**: Make strategy tangible and memorable
- **Time-boxed phases**: Balance thoroughness with speed

## Common Pitfalls

- Taking too long ("the strategy is coming")
- Skipping diagnosis phase
- Not involving stakeholders early enough
- Creating strategy without validating assumptions
- Treating process as linear when it's iterative

## Related Frameworks

- **Strategy Kernel**: Use as structure within these processes
- **Mission/Vision**: Part of strategy creation
- **Segmentation/Differentiation**: Key steps in strategy creation
- **Diagnosis Frameworks**: Tools for the diagnosis phase

## Example Use Cases

- **New Product**: Use 7-Step Playbook for rapid strategy creation
- **Major Strategy Revision**: Use Digible process for comprehensive 4-month planning
- **Customer-Centered Strategy**: Use HBS JTBD process when customer jobs are central
